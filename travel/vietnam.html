Vietnam: The Complete Adventure

Vietnam is a land of contrasts—ancient temples, buzzing cities, emerald rice fields, dramatic mountains, and stunning beaches. Here’s my comprehensive guide, packed with everything you need to know to plan your own unforgettable journey!

My 11-Day Vietnam Itinerary

  • Day 1: Ho Chi Minh City – Walking Tour, Cu Chi Tunnels, Nightlife at Phạm Ngũ Lão
  • Day 2: Mekong Delta – Day tour to My Tho, floating markets, return to Ho Chi Minh
  • Day 3: Da Nang & Hai Van Pass – Scenic drive to Hue, Thien Mu Pagoda, Royal Tombs, Perfume River
  • Day 4: Hue Citadel, Ba Na Hills or Hoi An via Marble Mountains & Lady Buddha
  • Day 5: Hoi An – Walking tour, cycling to beaches, lantern-lit evenings
  • Day 6: Ba Na Hills (Golden Bridge, French Village), return to Hoi An
  • Day 7: Hanoi – Old Quarter walking tour, temples, night market
  • Day 8: Halong Bay – Overnight cruise
  • Day 9: Ninh Binh – Bich Dong Pagoda, rice fields
  • Day 10: Trang An boat tour, Hoa Lu Ancient Capital, Bai Dinh Pagoda
  • Day 11: Mua Cave viewpoint, return home

Vietnam Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March–April) and Autumn (September–November) for pleasant weather.
  • Transport: Domestic flights, trains, buses, and Grab (ride-hailing). Scooters are popular for local travel.
  • Currency: Vietnamese Dong (VND). Cash is king, but cards are accepted in big cities.
  • Language: Vietnamese. English is common in tourist areas.
  • Food: Don’t miss pho, banh mi, fresh spring rolls, and local coffee!

Ho Chi Minh City (Saigon)

  • Cu Chi Tunnels: Explore the vast underground network used during the Vietnam War. Take a guided tour to crawl through tunnels and learn about guerrilla tactics.
  • War Remnants Museum: A sobering look at the Vietnam War’s impact on the country. Highly recommended for history buffs.
  • Notre-Dame Cathedral & Central Post Office: French colonial architecture in the city center.
  • Ben Thanh Market: Shop for souvenirs, clothes, and street food. Bargaining is expected.
  • Pham Ngu Lao & Bui Vien Street: The backpacker district for nightlife, bars, and people-watching.
  • Mekong Delta Day Trip: Book a tour to My Tho or Ben Tre for boat rides, floating markets, and coconut candy workshops. Cai Rang floating market in Can Tho is the largest and most photogenic.
Tip: Join a free walking tour in District 1 to cover the main sights and get local insights using Free Walking Tours. Use Grab for easy and cheap city transport.

Hue

  • Imperial City (Citadel): A UNESCO site, this sprawling complex was home to the Nguyen emperors. Combo tickets available for Citadel + Tombs.
  • Royal Tombs: Don’t miss the Tombs of Tu Duc, Khai Dinh (ornate, dark stonework), and Minh Mang (colorful, set by a lake). Each tomb is unique in style and history.
  • Thien Mu Pagoda: Iconic seven-story pagoda overlooking the Perfume River. Combine with a dragon boat ride for a scenic trip. Enjoy the susnet.
  • Perfume River: Take a sunset boat ride for beautiful city views.
  • Getting Around: Rickshaws and bicycles or scooty are fun ways to explore the city and riverside.
Tip: Buy the combo ticket for the Citadel and tombs to save money. Carry cash for entry fees.

Da Nang & Ba Na Hills

  • My Khe & Non Nuoc Beaches: Long stretches of white sand, perfect for relaxing or water sports or catching the sunset.
  • Lady Buddha: A giant Buddha statue with panoramic views and beautiful temple grounds.
  • Marble Mountains: Five limestone hills with caves, Buddhist shrines, and viewpoints. Entry ~40,000 VND.
  • Ba Na Hills & Golden Bridge: Ride the world’s longest cable car to a mountaintop theme park. The Golden Bridge, held by giant stone hands, is a must-see. Explore the French Village, gardens, pagodas, and amusement park rides. Buy tickets online to skip queues. Very very photogenic!
  • Hai Van Pass: One of Vietnam’s most scenic drives—hire a scooter or take a jeep tour for winding mountain and coastal views.
Tip: The Hai Van Pass connects Da Nang and Hue. You can send your luggage by bus and ride a scooter one way for the full experience.

Hoi An

  • Old Town: Wander lantern-lit streets, see ancient merchant houses, and enjoy riverside cafes. Entry ticket covers several sites.
  • Japanese Covered Bridge: The most famous landmark—take a photo or pay to walk across. Unfortunately it was under construction when we visited.
  • Assembly Halls: Visit the Fujian and Cantonese Assembly Halls for ornate temples and Chinese heritage.
  • Ba Mu Temple: Not as famous, but worth visiting for its unique three-gate entrance and peaceful pond.
  • Night Market: Open every evening—shop for souvenirs, try street food, and see the town glow with lanterns.
  • Cycling: Rent a bike to explore the countryside, rice paddies, and nearby An Bang or Cua Dai beaches. Bai Bien Cua Dai is quieter and less touristy.
  • Boat Ride: Float down the Thu Bon River at sunset in a lantern boat for magical views.
Tip: Join a free walking tour to cover the main sights. Try local dishes like cao lau and white rose dumplings.

Hanoi

  • Old Quarter: The heart of Hanoi—narrow streets, bustling markets, and endless street food. Don’t miss the weekend night market (Fri–Sun).
  • Hoan Kiem Lake & Ngoc Son Temple: A peaceful spot in the city center. Modest dress required for temple entry.
  • St. Joseph’s Cathedral: Neo-Gothic church inspired by Notre-Dame in Paris. Beautiful both inside and out.
  • Hanoi Train Street: Watch a train squeeze through a narrow residential lane—check timings and arrive early for a good spot.
  • Temple of Literature, One Pillar Pagoda, Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum: Iconic landmarks worth a visit.
  • Dong Xuan Market: Great for souvenirs and local snacks.
  • Water Puppet Show: Unique traditional theater, fun for all ages.
Tip: Stay in the Old Quarter for easy access to sights and food. Watch out for pickpockets in busy areas.

Halong Bay / Lan Ha Bay

  • Overnight Cruise: The best way to see the bay’s limestone karsts and islands. Choose from budget to luxury boats—most include meals, kayaking, and cave visits.
  • Cat Ba Island: Stay on the island for hiking, beaches, and a quieter vibe.
  • Activities: Kayaking, swimming, exploring caves, and sunrise on deck.
Tip: Book cruises in advance (especially in peak season). Bring a light jacket—it can get cool on the water.

Ninh Binh

  • Trang An Boat Tour: Glide through rivers and caves surrounded by karst mountains. Route 2 is the best balance of caves and temples, including a “Kong: Skull Island” filming site. Early morning or late afternoon trips to avoid crowds.
  • Bich Dong Pagoda: Picturesque bridge and cave temple. Climb up for hidden shrines and sunset views.
  • Mua Cave Viewpoint: Climb 500 steps for panoramic views of rice fields and rivers. Ignore aggressive parking touts—park at the official Ecolodge.
  • Bai Dinh Pagoda: Vietnam’s largest pagoda complex, with towering statues and temples.
  • Hoa Lu Ancient Capital: Explore remnants of Vietnam’s 10th-century capital—triple-arched gate, temples, and pagodas.
Tip: Stay in Tam Coc or Trang An for easy access to all sites. Rent a scooter or bicycle for local travel.

Essential Vietnam Experiences

  • Take a scooter/bike along the Hai Van Pass for epic coastal views.
  • Eat pho and banh mi from street stalls—Vietnamese food is cheap, fresh, and delicious everywhere.
  • Try Vietnamese coffee—strong, sweet, and often served with condensed milk or egg.
  • Join a free walking tour in any major city for local insights.
  • Book overnight trains or buses for long distances to save time and money.
